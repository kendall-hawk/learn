Introduction

Why do we teach children to be polite? Does the language we use really affect what people think of us? Neil and Beth discuss this and teach you some new vocabulary.

This week's question

What is the British idiom we use in certain situations to remind someone to be especially polite?

Choose one answer:

Listen to the programme to hear the answer.

Vocabulary

thingy
(informal) used if you can't remember someone's or something's name
innit
(slang) short form of 'isn't it' which can be used at the end of a statement for emphasis
insulting
rude or offensive
well disposed to (someone)
like or approve of (someone)
make excuses for (someone)
explain the reasons for someone else's (negative) behaviour
this and that
(idiom) various unspecified things
